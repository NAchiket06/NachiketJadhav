PROCEDURAL GENERATION

Procedural terrain generation in games involves the automatic creation of virtual landscapes through algorithms rather than handcrafting every detail. This approach not only saves time and resources but also provides players with unique and unpredictable landscapes, fostering a sense of discovery and exploration within the game.

PERLIN NOISE

Perlin noise has a great ability to generate coherent, flowing structures, making it valuable for simulating natural phenomena such as terrain landscapes, clouds, and organic textures. It is frequently employed in game development and computer graphics to add realism and complexity to procedural generation algorithms, providing a visually pleasing randomness that avoids abrupt changes and creates more visually appealing, organic patterns.
